# Nano Call - use cases

Nano Call does one thing: a **"Call us" button** on a website that rings a business live in the browser. Free, encrypted, nothing for the visitor to install. The business keeps the operator console open like a desk phone. Below are realistic ways to sell and place it.

## 1. "Call us now" button for a small business (the core sell)

- **Setup:** the business keeps the console open on their shop/office PC (online as e.g. `acme-plumbing`). Paste the embed snippet on their site.
- **Flow:** a visitor taps the floating button, picks a subject ("Quote", "Booking", "Support"), adds an optional note, and the office PC rings - with that context shown before they answer. No phone number dialled, no per-minute cost.
- **Why it sells:** almost no small business offers a one-click live voice button on their own domain. A feature you can add to every site you build.

## 2. Inline "talk to us" on a contact or pricing page

- **Setup:** drop the inline button (`<span data-nano-call-button>`) next to the address, in the contact section, or beside a pricing tier.
- **Flow:** instead of a phone number people have to dial, they click and talk immediately. The subject picker doubles as lightweight routing context ("Sales" vs "Support").

## 3. Service desk / reception line

- **Setup:** reception keeps the console open all day; subjects map to the things they handle ("New enquiry", "Existing order", "Complaint").
- **Flow:** the note + subject act as a mini intake form, so whoever answers already knows why the visitor is calling.

## 4. Campaign or event landing page

- **Setup:** put the floating button on a one-off landing page (a promotion, an open day, a launch) with a campaign-specific subject list.
- **Flow:** interested visitors reach a real person instantly during the campaign window, then the button comes down with the page.

## 5. Privacy-first contact channel

- **Setup:** a consultant, clinic, or tradesperson who would rather not publish a personal mobile number.
- **Flow:** visitors reach them directly on their own domain - no signup, no number, no call logs, audio encrypted end to end. Good for anyone who values keeping a third party out of the call.

## Where it is NOT the right tool (honest limits)

- **Reaching the business when the console is closed** - there is no app or push notification, so the operator must have the page open. It is a staffed-line tool, not voicemail.
- **The business answering on a mobile on the go** - phone browsers freeze background tabs, so run the console on an always-on desktop / shop PC.
- **Calling real phone numbers** - it is browser to browser only; reaching landlines/mobiles would need a paid SIP provider.
- **Visitor-to-visitor calls** - Nano Call is one-directional by design: visitors call the business, not each other.
